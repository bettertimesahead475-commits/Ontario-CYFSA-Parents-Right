â“ 10 Questions to Ask Every Lawyer

Ask these questions in your first consultation â€” before you hire anyone.

Question 1
"How many child protection cases have you handled under the CYFSA?"
Child protection law is specialized. You want someone with CYFSA experience specifically, not just general family law.
Question 2
"Have you appeared at [your local courthouse]? Do you know the judges and CAS workers there?"
Local knowledge is a significant advantage. Lawyers who know the local court culture can navigate more effectively.
Question 3
"What is your honest assessment of my case after reviewing these facts?"
A good lawyer gives you an honest assessment, not just what you want to hear. Beware of anyone who guarantees outcomes.
Question 4
"What is the least restrictive outcome we can realistically fight for?"
Under CYFSA s.1, courts prefer the least restrictive option. A good lawyer should be targeting supervision, not just fighting wardship.
Question 5
"Who will actually handle my case day-to-day â€” you or a junior lawyer?"
At some firms, senior lawyers sign up clients but juniors do the work. Know who you're actually getting.
Question 6
"How do you communicate with clients? How quickly will you return my calls and emails?"
Communication is critical in CAS cases. You need a lawyer who is responsive, especially around court dates and CAS visits.
Question 7
"What is your retainer and estimated total cost?"
Get cost estimates in writing. Child protection cases can run months or years â€” understand the financial commitment.
Question 8
"Will you attend every court appearance or send duty counsel?"
Your lawyer should appear at all significant hearings personally, not just send a substitute at the last minute.
Question 9
"Have you ever identified a Charter rights violation in a CAS case? What happened?"
This tells you if the lawyer thinks beyond standard procedure and understands constitutional arguments â€” often the strongest defense.
Question 10
"What should I do and not do between now and my next court date?"
A good lawyer gives you specific, practical guidance â€” not just general advice. Listen carefully and follow it exactly.
